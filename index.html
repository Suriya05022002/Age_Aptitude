Age Problems - Key Formulas

Basic Age Relationships

If A is x years older than B:

Age of A = Age of B + x

If A is x times as old as B:

Age of A = x × Age of B

Age Ratios

If ages of A and B are in ratio m:n:

Age of A / Age of B = m / n

If sum of ages is S:

Age of A = (m × S) / (m + n)

Future Ages

After t years:

New Age = Current Age + t

New ratio after t years:

(A + t) : (B + t)

Past Ages

t years ago:

Past Age = Current Age - t

Past ratio t years ago:

(A - t) : (B - t)

Question 1:

B is twice as old as A. C is twice as old as B. If the difference between ages of A and C is 12 years, find the age of B.

  • A) 8 years
  • B) 7 years
  • C) 6 years
  • D) 5 years
Explanation:

Let the ages of A, B and C be x, y and z.
y = 2x, z = 2y
z = 4x
Difference between ages of x and z = 12 years
4x – x = 12 years
3x = 12 years, x = 4 years
y = 2x = 2 * 4 = 8 years

y = 25 + 10 = 35 years
